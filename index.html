Simulation Guide
"This simulation demonstrates how simple evolutionary rules can produce intelligent behavior over time without explicit programming of solutions."
— Core Purpose of this Simulation
🔬 What This Simulation Is

A simplified model of evolution in a controlled digital environment. A group of organisms exists inside a 2D field, each attempting to reach a specific goal position.

Natural Selection Mutation Reproduction
RESULT The population gradually improves its ability to reach the goal over generations.
🌍 Elements of the Simulation
🧬 Organisms
  • Individual members of the population
  • Each acts as an independent agent
  • Shown as small glowing dots on the field
  • Will move and evolve over generations
Each organism carries a set of instructions (genes) that determine its behavior.
🎯 Goal State
  • The objective all organisms attempt to reach
  • Visually distinct and highlighted on the field
  • Can be repositioned by clicking or dragging
The closer an organism gets to the goal, the better its fitness score.
🧭 Simulation Field
  • The 2D environment where all organisms exist
  • Acts as the "world" for the experiment
  • No hard boundaries — fully open space
🎛 Control Panel Parameters
👥 Population Size
Number of organisms in the simulation.
↑ Larger → more diversity
↓ Smaller → faster, less varied
🧪 Mutation Rate
Probability of random changes in organisms.
↑ Higher → more randomness
↓ Lower → more stable
⏱ Generation Length
Steps each organism gets per generation.
↑ Longer → more time to reach goal
↓ Shorter → quicker evaluation
⚖️ Selection Pressure
How many organisms survive each generation.
↑ High → only best survive
↓ Low → more survive, slower gain
📊 Stats Panel Explained
👥 Population Total organisms currently in the system
🏆 Best Fitness Score of the best performer — based on distance to goal
🔁 Generation Current iteration of the simulation cycle
🧠 Conceptual Flow
Initialize Population
↓
Organisms Attempt to Reach Goal
↓
Evaluate Fitness
↓
Select Best Performers
↓
Reproduce + Mutate
↓
Next Generation → Repeat
🧪 Your Role

You are the experiment controller. You adjust parameters, change the goal position, and observe system behavior. You are not controlling organisms directly — you control the conditions under which evolution occurs.

⚙️ Why This Is Interesting
Simple rules create complex behavior
Systems improve without direct control
Randomness + selection leads to intelligence
Intelligence can emerge from iteration, not instruction.
